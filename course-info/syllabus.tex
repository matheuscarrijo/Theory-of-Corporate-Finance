\begin{frame}\frametitle{Course Objective}

\begin{itemize}
\item Introducing master and doctoral students to (part of the) theoretical research in corporate finance
\item Emphasis: general equilibrium and contract theory with incomplete information
\item Fundamentals of corporate finance theory
    \begin{itemize}
    \item firm's choice of its capital structure and dividend policy under alternative assumptions
    \item moral hazard and agency problems, adverse selection and signalling
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

%------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{frame}\frametitle{Pre-requisites}

\begin{itemize}
\item Basic notions of general equilibrium and asset pricing
\item Non-cooperative game theory with and without incomplete information
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

%--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{frame}\frametitle{Indicative reading}

The text book for the course is
\begin{itemize}
\item \alert{Tirole, J.: 2006, ``The Theory of Corporate Finance, Princeton University Press'', Princeton and Oxford}
\end{itemize}

Some additional material can be found in
\begin{itemize}
\item Hart, O. D.: 1995, ``Firms Contracts and Finance Structure'', Oxford University Press
\item Smith, C. W.: 1990, ``The Modern Theory of Corporate Finance'', McGraw Hill-Irwin
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

%--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{frame}\frametitle{Other material}

Another Ph.D. level text book is
\begin{itemize}
\item de Matos, J. A.: 2002, ``Theoretical Foundations of Corporate Finance'', Princeton University Press
\end{itemize}

There are many good MBA text books

\begin{itemize}
\item Copeland, T., Weston, J. and Shastri: 2005, ``Financial Theory and Corporate Policy'', Pearson Addison-Wesley
\item Grinblatt, M. and Titman, S.: 1998, ``Financial Markets and Corporate Strategy'', Irwin-McGraw-Hill
\end{itemize}

The standard text book on corporate finance at the MBA level is
\begin{itemize}
\item Brealey, R. A., Myers, S. C. and F., A.: 2008, ``Principles of Corporate Finance'', McGraw Hill-Irwin
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}
